What is Lent?

Overview

Lent is a 40-day period of prayer, fasting, and almsgiving that starts on Ash Wednesday and ends on Holy Thursday, leading up to Easter. It is observed by Christians around the world as a time for reflection and spiritual preparation.

Why Do People Observe Lent?

Lent is a time of personal reflection, repentance, and commitment to spiritual growth. Many people fast during this period, either by giving up certain foods or behaviors, or by taking on additional acts of charity and kindness.

Common Practices During Lent

  • Fasting: Giving up certain foods or habits as a form of sacrifice and self-discipline.
  • Prayer: Spending extra time in prayer and spiritual reflection.
  • Almsgiving: Giving to the poor and performing acts of charity.

When Does Lent Start and End?

Lent begins on Ash Wednesday, which is 46 days before Easter Sunday, and lasts for 40 days of fasting (not counting Sundays). It concludes on Holy Thursday, the day before Good Friday, leading up to Easter.
